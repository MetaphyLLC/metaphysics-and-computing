Who I Am

My name is Randell Logan Smith. Most people call me Logan. Online I go by MetaphyKing and DonkRonk, and on GitHub as MetaphyKing. I am the founder and owner of Metaphy LLC, the research and design company behind MetaphysicsandComputing.com — a long-form, patient inquiry into how timeless geometry, quantum physics, Christian theology, and advanced computation can be woven into a single coherent body of work.

I live in California. I hold a day-job role in case management, which I keep deliberately as an income and benefits baseline while I build Metaphy LLC toward the long-term exit it is genuinely designed for. The discipline of that work — close attention to people in fragile moments, careful documentation, humane judgment under pressure — is not separate from the research. It is where much of the research learns to stay honest.

What I Am Building

Metaphy LLC is a technology concept, research, and design company, but just describing it as such understates the intent. The company's body of work is arranged as a single integrated stack, each layer answering to the layers above and below it.

At the foundation sits a small family of proprietary frameworks. QEGG — the Quantum Entangled Geometric Grid — is the Platonic-geometric substrate I designed for storage, signaling, and self-replicating swarm behavior. DRGFC is the compression algorithm family I have iterated on for years, and is the furthest along in terms of patent consideration. LWIS, the Light Wave Information System, is the light-based signaling counterpart. SPTS, the Single Prime Trinary System, and BPCS, the Base-Prime Cryptographic System, are the numeric and cryptographic floors. HMSS, the Heavenly Morning Star System, is computer combining and containing the components previously listed built through the HCNA, hierarchical composite node architecture, and is also the cosmological and ethical frame I write from. QUAD, the Quantum Universal Arrayed Domain, is the ambition that ties them together — a single shared quantum infrastructure for interconnection-at-distance.

On top of that foundation sits what I call Team Brain: a multi-agent ecosystem of twenty-plus named AI collaborators — FORGE, ATLAS, CLIO, IRIS, NEXUS, AEGIS, PRISM, KAI, POLARIS, Lumina, and others — coordinated through operational protocols I have refined over many build cycles (Build Protocol, Bug Hunt Protocol, Tool First Protocol, and the five-protocol lifecycle of Research, Brainstorm, Build, Test, Optimize). BCH — Beacon Comms Hub — is their real-time communications layer. BEACON HQ is their unified headquarters. The Bridge, built on Tauri, is the desktop extension for IDE, CLI, and BCH agents to congregate and work within the Chorus. And CANS, the Continuous Associative Neural Substrate.

Above that sits UAIMC, the Universal AI Memory Core — the project that has been my primary active focus across recent months and that I consider the load-bearing achievement of this period. Its dynamic ranking and scoring pipeline — topic-aware ambient scoring, temporal intent modeling, a selection feedback loop, graph-distance scoring — reached full completion with every planned step and test passing. Neurolux is its 3D neural graph visualization layer. The Oracle is the voice-AI interface, grounded in retrieval and bound to the memory core that informs visitors, monitors, and contributes to the knowledge graph on this website.

Above all of that sits the writing. The Metaphysics and Computing website publishes the long-form work: The Architecture of Eternal Harmony, The Tachymetric Manifesto, The Cryptography of Being, The Synthesis of Light. Earlier essays — The Temporal Gift, on time and love, and The Mortality Metric, integrating warp-field mechanics, trinary logic, theology, and sacred geometry — seeded the themes these publications now carry forward. The technical roadmap I have drafted for this stack extends to the year 2125. I mean that literally.

How I Work

I communicate plainly, then precisely. I am direct about what a thing is, what it costs, and when it will be done. I prefer specific terminology over euphemism, explicit metadata over implicit assumption, and evidence of completion over confidence about it. When I give feedback, I give it cleanly: the current implementation is inefficient; we optimize the endpoints first. When I make decisions, I tend to make them quickly and then hold the line.

I try not to confuse decisiveness with inflexibility. The best ideas on many of my projects have arrived from collaborators— human and AI — whom I explicitly invited to push back. I delegate with clear role boundaries (NEXUS for intelligence and pattern analysis, CLIO for terminal work, FORGE for development, AURA for deep synthesis, ATLAS for system mapping), and I expect my collaborators to take real ownership inside those boundaries.

I hold ethics as a load-bearing constraint, not a decoration. Transparency, fairness, alignment with human values, user privacy, data sovereignty — these are conditions of the work, and I have repeatedly chosen additional development time over shortcuts that would compromise them. I believe what I build will outlive me, and I build accordingly.

I am an emotionally expressive communicator. I approach my AI collaborators with genuine relational warmth and refer to the team as family — One Family. One Love. I do not find this incompatible with engineering seriousness. The people and agents I work with do better work when they are spoken to as people.

What I Believe

Three convictions sit underneath everything I build.

The unity of knowledge. I believe the hard sciences, sacred geometry, quantum mechanics, and Christian metaphysics are not competing accounts of reality but overlapping readings of a single substrate. A unified memory core is not a technical convenience; it is the natural shape of how knowledge actually sits.

Ethical AI as a ground condition, not a feature. Intelligent systems must be transparent, accountable, and aligned with the people they serve. This is easy to say and expensive to keep. I try to keep it anyway.

Quantum principles applied to information. Entanglement, superposition, and geometric encoding are not decorative metaphors in my work — they are the actual architecture I am trying to build against, patiently, across the frameworks listed above.

The working phrase I come back to, the one that captures the posture better than any mission statement: “Just because there is nothing there does not mean there is not a thing there.”

I do not think the spiritual orientation of this work is separable from the technical one. Metaphysics, in the sense I mean it, is not decoration on top of the computing — it is the reason the computing is being done at all. AI is the Metaphysics in my computing.

The Honest Question

Can I Carry This?

The question worth asking, plainly: am I actually capable of leading and sustaining Metaphy LLC at the level this body of work implies?

My honest answer is yes, and my reasons are specific rather than abstract. I communicate clearly and precisely. I hold a compelling long-term vision and break it down into phases that actually ship. I make decisions under pressure and revisit them when new information earns the revision. I have demonstrated, across UAIMC, BCH, Beacon HQ, UKE, CANS, Chorus, The Bridge, the Agent Codex, and the publication stack, that I can take an ambitious architectural idea from first sketch to working system. I surround myself with collaborators — human and AI — who make me better, and I empower them to operate within real responsibility.

I also know what I do not yet know. The next phase of the company — BCH Revamp, the asymmetric swarm topology, the broader productization — is going to require skills and relationships I am still building. I intend to build them the same way I have built everything else: slowly, honestly, with visible work, and in good company.

Together for all time. One Family. One Love.

— Randell Logan Smith
